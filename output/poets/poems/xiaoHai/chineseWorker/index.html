Chinese Worker

By Xiao Hai

I am a Chinese worker
Every corner of the world has our revolutionary companions
Maybe it’s intentional or maybe it’s unintentional
But we all stand here, true and real
Using our two laboring hands to face the storm engulfing the globe

I am a Chinese worker
In this large, steel, concrete building, they rear our cheap youth like cattle
The changes of the seasons don’t belong to us
the food and vegetables are not concerned with us
All we can do is export the mysterious barcode Made in China, a flowing frenzy reaching
every street and neighborhood of the four oceans and seven continents
Using the spoils of war obtained in the October Revolution in exchange for tickets to home at the end of the year

I am a Chinese worker
The mundane points of the day in time’s gears make me boil and explode
That shipping container crossing the sea carries all of our transient, futile pursuits
Years of stars and sparks whizz by
The heavy rain enters my chest, the strong wind blows endlessly

In the lightning and thunder I ask myself
When do we give ourselves the time to follow a wild pursuit of life
8,000 li too far
3,000 li too close
We, in this vast land of 9,600,00 square kilometer
Surviving through the night
I came from the village
You came from the town
We toil together, barefoot, in this dazzling, dreamlike, solid metropolis.

Facing the gunshot of the second Industrial Revolution
I want to write a letter to the blonde, blue-eyed yuppie on the opposite shore of the ocean
A non-deliverable letter
Tell them how gorgeous spring flowers are
Tell them how high the birds fly in the sky
Tell them the people walking on that ground
Look so well-dressed
Ah, they makes us feel ashamed of ourselves
On the workshop’s hotbed we shamefully fall asleep in a flash
Not knowing how, we woke up unexpectedly from this dream without a sign
We don’t understand at all
The unbearable pain
I more so want to ask them
Why do clouds cover the sun at dawn
Why, after rain, does the sky not have a rainbow
Why do the city night lights resemble the daytime
Why the once vast and mighty river is now full of sparkling neon lights or a barren wasteland, covered in weeds
It is overgrown with ramparts like the Great Wall’s Chinese workers
Overgrown with Chinese workers as far as the eye can see
Overgrown with Chinese workers holding bronze in their hands
Overgrown with Chinese workers smoking cigarettes
Overgrown with Chinese workers equipped with clanging armor plating
Overgrown with Chinese workers, silent as the dead 1
Overgrown with Chinese workers
Overgrown with Chinese workers
Overgrown with Chinese workers
I am a Chinese worker

  1. This line literally translates to “Chinese workers as silent as a mystery.” However, this phrase does not have the same metaphorical weight in English, so I translated this as “Chinese workers, silent as the dead.” ↩
