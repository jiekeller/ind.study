Poets

Throughout this project, I translated works from several different migrant worker poets. Click on a poet to view translations of their poems.
Chen Nianxi

A demolition worker from Shaanxi, Chen Nianxi's poetry often reflects on the migrant workers all over the country.

Li Ruo

Li Ruo moved to North over ten years ago to find work, she is a current member of the Picun Writers' Group.

Qi Feng Chi

A male migrant worker poet based in Hebei.

Wang Jibing

A delivery-man poet from Shanghai, many of his poems are concise and describe the difficulties of delivery work.

Wan Huashan

Born in Henan, Wan Huashan has worked many laboring jobs. He currently lives in Beijing as a writer.

Wu Xia

A garment worker in Shenzhen, Wu Xia writes of the freedom and wealth she wishes she had.

Xiao Hai

Graduating from the Chinese department of Nanjing University, Xiao Hai has published many poems, containing ambiguous and wispy language.

Xu Duo

A migrant worker songwriter and poet, Xu Duo's poetry is positive and speaks to the collective spirit of Chinese migrant workers.

Xu Lizhi

A Foxconn worker from Shenzhen, Xu Lizhi's poetry is dark and graphic, reflective of his beliefs towards Chinese society.
