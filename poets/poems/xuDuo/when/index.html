When

By Xu Duo

When love leaves us behind
what am I supposed to do?
Let us sing a joyful song
Although society is cold and unjust
this spring is splendid and brilliant

When youth leaves us behind
what am I supposed to do?
Let us put down that liquor and smoke
Although society is cold and unjust
life still has many roads yet to be traveled

When dreams leave us behind
what am I supposed to do?
Even if discouraged, live on
Either run away, or lift my head up to keep going
Searching the earth for new roads and laughter

When choices leaves us behind
what am I supposed to do?
Caught in the flow of time
Although society is cold and unjust
you can still find company in misery

Ah—

When love leaves us behind
what am I supposed to do?
Let us sing the song Lucky as Shit
Although society is cold and unjust
996 requires a grateful heart

When communication leaves us behind
what am I supposed to do?
Let us explode or die in silence?
Although society is cold and unjust
we still want to communicate

When dreams leave us behind
what am I supposed to do?
Even if discouraged, live on
Either run away, or lift my head up to keep going
Searching the earth for new roads and laughter

When choices leaves us behind
what am I supposed to do?
With the ups and downs of this destined era
Although society is cold and unjust
you can still find company in misery

To meet others with this shared fate
Let us sing this song together!
In this poem, Xu Duo articulates the collective strength in unity of migrant workers, reciting a rhythmic, song-like pattern. When something so natural and fundamental, such as love, youth or choice leaves him and his fellow workers behind, he ruminates on what to do next. Xu Duo is well aware of the challenges and barriers keeping him down in this world (such as the demanding '996' work schedule), yet he maintains an unwavering positivity, relying on his compatriots to derive strength and union. As he recites at the end of the poem, “Although society is cold and unjust [y]ou can still find company in misery [t]o meet others with this shared fate [l]et us sing this song together!”
